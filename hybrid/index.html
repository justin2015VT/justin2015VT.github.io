Winter at Virginia Tech



Snowball Fight

Snowball Fight, 2016

The “war” starts off relatively the same each year. The civilians gather on the Drill Field, temporarily taking ownership of the land before the invasion. Hundreds of warriors on both sides prepare snowballs. From the top of the Drill Field next to the Pylon, the enemy advances with a war cry and a wave of snowballs. The civilians race to counteract the assault. It's a brutal battle and I am pesonally grateful that similar to video games with unlimited lives, if you get hit with a snowball you aren’t kicked out of the fight. I would have been kicked out before the first wave made it to our frontlines.




Events of that Day

The battle raged as the front lines crashed into one another, snowballs flying. The photo to the left makes it look like we were outnumbered, but reinforcements were not far off. They were, however, taking their time. Maybe if the guy in blue would stop be astonished by his own snowball, we’d be winning. In his defense, that is a nice-looking snowball, but, there is no time for this. Man, this is a war. Follow the lady in red. She knows how to fight.














I think he’s lost it. He just can’t handle it. The stresses of war is to much and he’s resorted to the only thing that makes any sense: stripping down and making a snow angel. Clearly, he thinks he can distract the enemy. The only thing is, this photo was taken before the war began. Maybe it’s some technique to prepare. If you can’t feel the cold, you can’t feel the snowball.

Okay, so the truth. This guy is my roommate. I guess the only logical question is, was this a dare? The answer is, no. Is he supporting anything? Again, no. He just thought it would be fun to do, so he did it. He also wanted to be videotaped and so, somehow I got recruited for the job which meant, for fifteen minutes, I was running after a half-naked guy with a video recorder. I can’t help but wonder what all the bystanders thought.













Victory! They snowball fight of 2016 belongs to the civilians. We outnumbered the enemy, 3:1. We surrounded them and pushed them off the Drill Field. They had nowhere else to go, unlike myself. War makes me hungry, especially a victorious one, and so you can see me leaving in the photo on the left. You can still see the crowd in the background. As for everyone else headed to the Drill Field, they remind me of the scene in Pirates of the Carribean.

You may recall that in the movie, the Governor takes refuge in his cabin while the ship is being ambushed. He emerges at the end of fight and cheers with the victors. As you can see, however, I’m covered in snow. Those walking behind me are not. They can fool themselves, but they can’t fool the true warriors.

Other Winter Events

Jeans that Stand

It was a Monday in January. Classes hadn't started and I was bored. Snow had been steadily falling all day and so I turned to YouTube to pass the time. Tired of just throwing snow, I looked for other things I could do with the cold atmosphere and stumbled upon a YouTube video (select inspiration link below to see a similar video).

Now, know that I was not about to stand in the cold with wet jeans. I was bored, but not that bored. So, sticking with the Virginia Tech motto of Invent the Future, I stuffed the pants with blankets, looped coat hangers around the belt of the pants, and inserted a wooden bar through the hanger and left the jeans outside all night.

The temperature stayed in the teens. The following morning, I removed the hangers and blankets, but to my dismay, I had some drunk pants. They tried to stand but kept falling over. The lesson: next time, just man up and soak the jeans, stand out all night in the freezing weather and get some sober pants. Or I guess I can just wait until it is even colder.

Boiling Water

The Saturday following my failed jeans experiment, I found myself searching YouTube again. The time was roughly around eight and I still hadn’t made up my mind on whether or not I was going out that night. Snow was still everywhere and so my roommates had already opted to stay in.

That was when I found the video below (just select the inspiration tab). I know it doesn’t look like much, but it was something to do so I began the “challenging” task of boiling water. At the time, I didn’t realize the real challenge was going to be transferring the water from the stove outside. I spilt some on my hand. I was okay, snow was nearby.

It worked. The weather was only in teens and the water smoke travelled from the apartment over the grass and onto the main road. To the people driving by, sorry!

Sunset

Tech always has the most beautiful sceneries, even in a slush filled parking lot of an apartment complex. There isn’t much else like a brisk walk around town at sunset after a snowfall. This particular sunset occurred on the same day as my failed jeans experiment. Even though my car was covered in snow and I had a failed experiment, Tech and Blacksburg always knows how to cheer me up. Go, Hokies!
